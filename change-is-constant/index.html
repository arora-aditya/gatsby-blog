Change is the only constant

December 23, 2019 • ☕️ 4 min read

PersonalReflection

As September rolled around, I clearly remember the first day when I moved to Toronto, when a good friend (who was also moving to Toronto) expressed his concern about the change - living in a big city all by himself for the first time. At that point, I was just happy that I was out of Waterloo, after having done all previous study and work terms in that tiny city alone, I was wary but I was happy to get a change of environment and get out of the rut I was in.

As time passed, and I started my new internship, things felt different yet same. I was on a similar work schedule at a similar company only this time I was in a big city surrounded by way more people than I was used to. This was also the first time where I was rooming with my fellow intern from work, Rock Zhou.

Living in Toronto, commuting was also different yet similar. For the first two and a half months or so, with amazing weather we [Rock and I] walked to work, and once the weather got frigid we started to take the bus to work. That daily commute is where rooming with Rock helped, since we were able to talk about school, work and everything in between. As for commuting in Toronto, my favourite part of it was the subway system because its - independent of the weather, fairly reliable, very frequent and covers most of the hot-spots on Yonge St and Bloor St! Despite commuting to work in a bus, my favourite mode of transport is now definitely the subway system.

Another change that started this term, was me eating better. As I turned twenty this September, my personal resolution to myself definitely played a role in me getting up early and making breakfast. Working at KitchenMate also played a role in this, since I was easily able to get healthy and ready to eat meals at a price that I could afford as a student in a city as expensive as Toronto!

The work that I was doing was also familiar yet different, the web and data tasks I did were familiar, and the embedded systems tasks were where I felt I was learning and changing the most. I felt that the work I did was by far the most impactful of my past internships, and having a say in improving the software process definitely made me feel empowered!

Since it was also both Rock and I’s first time in Toronto, weekends were spent trying out new places and visiting new spots in Toronto. We visited a bunch of new food spots, went to the graffiti alley and the Christmas market to take some dope pictures, and also saw the the Christmas parade in Toronto! This was also a welcome change compared to the ~5 good spots in a town where nothing interesting happens [i.e. Waterloo], all of which have been tried and tested and dusted over the past two years.

Over the term I spent some time learning about databases, ray tracing all while doing weekly leetcode contests, and for the first time taking part in the annual advent of code contest [blog coming soon]. I tried to spend some more time this term trying to learn new technologies and concepts.
